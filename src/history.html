History of Apple's CEOs

People think Steve Jobs was the CEO of apple since the beginning, but the first CEO was Michael Scott from 1977 to 1981. Michael Scott was named CEO because the other workers were too inexperienced.

The second CEO was an investor by the name Mike Markkula. Markkula was Apple's third employee who served as CEO from 1981 to 1983. Even with Steve Jobs high graces, Markkula had Steve Jobs removed from the company.

The third CEO was a former PepsiCo CEO named John Sculley. John Sculley was Apple's CEO from 1983 to 1993. Sculley had great marketing skills to help sell computers but the company suffered high costs during his time as CEO.

The fourth CEO was Michael Spindler who was knicknamed "The Diesel". Spindler was CEO from 1993 to 1996 where he was blamed for the failure of Newton and Copland operating systems.

The fifth CEO was Gil Amelio who was the CEO of National Semiconductor previously. Amelio introduced Mac OS 8, but was soon removed when the company's stock was at a 12 year low.

Finally the sixth CEO was the one and only Steve Jobs who remained CEO from 1997 to 2011 where he stepped down because of health issues. Steve Jobs helped make Apple profitable again and revolutionized the technology world. Steve Jobs helped create Mac OS X, iPod, iPhone, and iPad along with improvements with the iMac.

The seventh and present CEO of Apple is Tim Cook who took over when Steve Jobs stepped down. Cook is known for being a quiet man. Cook is in great position to help Apple innovate and become even greater than before.

Did you know..

Jobs and Wozniak named the company Apple because they were major Beatles fans.
